## New Biome Design Plan {#new-biome-design-plan}  
Designing a new biome involves a structured approach to balance ecological, aesthetic, and functional elements. The process begins with **terrain analysis**, where geological features, elevation changes, and landforms are mapped to create a foundational structure. This includes decisions about mountain ranges, valleys, and water bodies to shape the biome’s physical character.  

Next, **climate considerations** define temperature ranges, precipitation patterns, and seasonal variations. These factors determine vegetation types, weather dynamics, and how the biome interacts with its environment. For example, a tropical biome would prioritize high humidity and consistent rainfall, while a tundra biome would focus on cold temperatures and minimal snowfall.  

**Wildlife integration** ensures ecological balance by selecting native species and designing habitats that meet their survival needs. This includes food sources, shelter, and migration pathways. Care must be taken to avoid overpopulation or resource competition, maintaining a sustainable ecosystem.  

Finally, the design process incorporates iterative testing and adjustments, ensuring the biome aligns with its intended purpose—whether for gameplay, education, or environmental simulation.
